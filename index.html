DualGaussians: A Hybrid Dual Gaussian Representation for Reconstruction and Rendering of Dynamic Objects from Monocular Video

Weixing Xie, Ying Ye, Xian Wu, Jintian Li, Junfeng Yao*
Bingchuan Li, Yong Yang, Zhongquan Jian, Xiao Dong
Xiamen University

Framework of our DualGaussians.

Abstract

Reconstructing high-fidelity and temporally consistent meshes from monocular video is a challenging task. 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) revolutionized radiance field reconstruction due to its advantages in novel view synthesis and rendering speed. However, due to the multi-view inconsistency nature of 3DGS, it is not suitable for reconstructing the geometry of objects. In contrast, 2DGS yields smoother and more accurate surfaces, but suffers from restricted color modeling capabilities. To address these limitations, we propose DualGaussians, a novel hybrid framework that jointly leverages 2DGS and 3DGS through mutual guidance and supervision, achieving superior geometry and appearance reconstruction. Specifically, we first train a dynamic 2DGS to obtain stable geometry and deformation field. We design gradient-aware normal consistency loss and pseudo-view depth consistency loss to improve geometric accuracy. Then our method initiates the training of 3DGS, using 2DGS and its deformations to guide the 3DGS. We design a refinement module to fine-tune the deformation to capture fine-grained time-varying appearance. We also propose a multi-level depth normalization to optimize the 3D Gaussian distribution using the depth of 2DGS. Moreover, the high-quality rendering of 3DGS can be used to suppress the floating noise in 2DGS and further improve the mesh quality. We conduct extensive experiments to demonstrate that DualGaussians achieves state-of-the-art performance, outperforming existing approaches in both geometry accuracy and visual fidelity.
